Today, we serve businesses across 48 states — from local storefronts to national franchise rollouts. Every sign we build carries our promise of quality, precision, and durability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our services and process

How long does a typical sign project take? ▼
Most standard projects are completed within 5-10 business days from design approval. Complex 3D fabrication or large-scale installations may take 2-4 weeks. Rush options are available — just let us know your timeline when you submit your order.
Do you handle permits and city approvals? ▼
Yes! We handle sign permit applications for most jurisdictions. Our team prepares engineering drawings, site plans, and all required documentation. Permit timelines vary by city — typically 1-4 weeks. We'll keep you updated throughout the process.
What materials do you use for outdoor signs? ▼
We use commercial-grade materials rated for outdoor exposure: aluminum (0.063" - 0.125"), acrylic (3mm - 25mm), HDU foam, PVC, dibond, polycarbonate, and stainless steel. All finishes include automotive-grade paint or powder coating with UV clear coats for maximum durability.
Do you ship nationwide? ▼
Absolutely! We ship to all 48 contiguous states. Large signs are crated and shipped via freight carriers (LTL). Smaller items ship via UPS or FedEx. Orders over $2,500 qualify for free shipping. Every shipment includes full insurance and tracking.
Can I see a proof before production? ▼
Yes — we never go to production without your approval. You'll receive a digital proof and/or 3D mockup rendering showing exactly how your sign will look. We include up to 2 rounds of revisions in our standard pricing. Additional revisions are available at our hourly design rate.
What's your warranty policy? ▼
All fabricated signs include a 3-year warranty covering manufacturing defects, LED failure, and finish issues. Our LEDs carry a separate 5-year performance warranty. Vehicle wraps are warranted for the life of the vinyl (typically 5-7 years for premium cast vinyl). We stand behind everything we build.
What file formats do you accept for printing? ▼
We accept AI, EPS, PDF, PSD, and SVG for vector artwork. For raster/photo images, we need high-resolution files at 150+ DPI at final size (TIFF, PSD, or JPEG). Our design team can also work from rough sketches, photos, or brand guidelines to create production-ready artwork.
